I read a cool book, September 14, 2022

Today I finished reading Chaos: Making a New Science by James Gleick. My granddad gave me this copy earlier this year and told me that I "have to read it if I am going to be a scientist." I don't really plan on being a scientist, but this book was inspiring, nevertheless.

Originally published in 1987, it discusses the breakthroughs in understanding quote "the irregular unpredictable behavior of deterministic, nonlinear dynamical systems." I'll try to describe what I think this means a bit later. It is remarkable to think about the state of science 35 years ago, and this book brought up some interesting ideas that I think we really take for granted today.
